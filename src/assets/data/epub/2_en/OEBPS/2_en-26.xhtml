The beauty of seal cutting lies in the beauty of calligraphy, composition and carving skills. The first two are both related to calligraphic accomplishment, but the latter reflects the unique appeal of seal cutting and the key to transforming calligraphy into seals. A sculptor uses carving knives, just as a calligrapher uses writing brush. Everyone knows that different kinds of knives, blunt or sharp, flat or oblique, are used for different carving techniques. Consequently, the finished seals are also different. However, not everyone is aware that seals can also be affected by different materials. The combination between carving knives and seal stones, just like that between brush and paper, needs to be fully understood and used carefully. Seal stones are tender or crisp, resilient or

soft. Seal cutters should choose different stones according to different calligraphic, composition and carving styles. They can carve freely only when the stone matches the carving knife, just as horses gallop unrestrainedly on the grassland. When the stone suits the carving knife and the hands follow mental signals, a seal cutter can enjoy the ultimate freedom in the world working with a small piece of stone.
