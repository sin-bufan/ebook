Only when you see and touch it personally, can you truly understand its intriguing, intoxicating beauty. There are various kinds of Shoushan stones. Traditionally, they are sorted into field, water and mountainous pits as their sources of origin. In recent years, some have categorized Shoushan stones according to the mountain systems of the mines into the Mt. Gaoshan System, Mt. Yueyang System, and Mt. Qijiang System. Each system is further divided into sub-categories. In the time of Emperor Qianlong, a saying went that there were three kinds of precious seal stones, of which the Tianhuang and Lotus stones both came from Shoushan. However, Shoushan is home to more precious stones such as Duling, Shanbo, Lizhi, Taizhi, Qijiang, Gaoshan, and Yuewei, each having its own unique characteristics. Despite their uniqueness, every one of these types differs from those produced elsewhere and enjoys a commonality of a Shoushan stones. However, this cannot be appreciated until one has

had close contact with Shoushan stones for years.

The beauty of the Shoushan stone lies in its exquisite craftsmanship. Shoushan Stone Carving is unique. Sculptors cannot arbitrarily show off their skills on the stone. Due to its unparalleled beauty and scarcity, sculptors must take into account its unique texture before beginning to carve. Hence, some claim that sculptors must “examine the stone” before carving, and the examination process is even of “nine-fold importance to the carving itself”. An excellent Shoushan stone carver never shows off his or her technique, but focuses on awakening the stone’s own beauty as much as possible. Compared with jade carving, Shoushan Stone Carving has a shorter history. Even so, it has undergone several historical phases. The stone was first used to carve burial objects in the Song Dynasty and then seals by scholars in the Ming Dynasty.
