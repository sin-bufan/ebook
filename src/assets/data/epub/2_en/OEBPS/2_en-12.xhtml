Compared with the prosperity and achievements of various seal-cutting genres since the Ming Dynasty, the recognition and research of seal stones and seal knob culture has been insufficient, meager and unfair in the past. Most experienced seal knob carvers did not have their names imprinted in history, except for only a few lucky ones such as Yang Yuxuan, Zhou Shangjun, and Lin Qingqing. What a huge regret and grievance!

The research of seal stone culture should start with analysis of basic materials, including both textual information and cultural relics. One can undertake thorough research only when one takes in all existing materials and continues exploring for more. For instance, seal stones were widely used by people from all walks of life in the Qing Dynasty, emperors being no exception. Seal cutting played a crucial role in their cultural activities.
