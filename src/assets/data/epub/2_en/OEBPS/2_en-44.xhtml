The precious and elegant Balin stone thus entered the royal court. There is much folklore about the stone, such as “Goddess Nuwa patching the sky with magic stones”, “Goddess of Pomegranate turning into stone”, “Phoenix spitting gemstones” and “Genghis Khan praising the Heaven-given stone”. Thus, Balin stone enjoys a great reputation, including “Apple of Emperor’s Eyes”, “Shining Pearl of the Southern Desert”. “Gem of Grassland”, “Ethnic Flower”, “King of Auspicious (similar pronunciation as King of Chicken-Blood Stone)”, “Queen of Good Luck (similar pronunciation as Fuhuang Stone),” and “Secrets of Wealth” (because a piece of Balin chicken-blood stone can make one rich and a piece of Balin Fuhuang Stone can buy a villa). From remote antiquity to today, the Balin stone has been a carrier of wealth and civilization.

In 1973, under the instruction of Premier Zhou Enlai,

Balin stone began to be mined. Upon the authentication of the industrial authorities, the Gems & Jewelry Trade Association of China and relevant specialists, the stone was named Balin after its birthplace according to unwritten routines. It has since been ranked among the Four Famous Stones of China, together with Shoushan stone, Changhua stone, and Qingtian stone. The Four Famous Stones compete with and complement each other, comprising the brilliant stone culture of China. Especially after China implemented its reform and opening up in the late 1970s, the Balin stone cultural industry grew ever stronger, and is playing a major role on the stage of the new century.

The Balin stone features various categories and colors. Based on its seven basic colors including red, orange, yellow, green, bluish, blue, and purple, it can create a plethora of colors that are deep, light, dark, bright, or mixed.
