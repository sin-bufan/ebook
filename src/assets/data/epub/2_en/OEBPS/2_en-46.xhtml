The third category is Dongshi (literally, “jelly stone”). It was thus named due to its jelly-like appearance. The stone is rich in pholerite, but has few coloring elements and admixtures, so it looks translucent, with a high degree of transparency. All transparent, semi-transparent or slightly transparent Balin stones that neither contain mercury sulfide nor are in yellow color can be categorized as Dongshi. Balin Dongshi can be divided into 38 sub-categories including Taohuadong, Shuijingdong, Meiguidong, Furongdong, Yangzhidong, and Shuicaodong.

The fourth category is Colored Stone. The stone is rich in kaolinite, as well as coloring elements and admixtures. Unlike chicken-blood stones and Dongshi, it is smooth but not transparent. Some are in pure, single color, and others feature mixed colors. Although the chicken-blood stone, Fuhuang stone and Dongshi all have colors, the Colored Stone refers to those with mixed colors. Balin Colored Stone can be divided into 23 sub-categories including Zhushashi, Kafeishi, Ziyunshi, Cibaishi, and Honghuashi.

The fifth category is the Pattern Stone. It refers to sectioned and polished Balin stones with beautiful natural patterns. Such patterns can be roughly categorized into animals, plants, figures, and landscapes. They look like beautiful colored photos printed on Balin stones. Seeing those lifelike patterns, provoke awe at the magic power of Nature.
