Appreciation and Appraisal of Changhua Stone

By Qian Gaochao

The Changhua stone is a kind of polymineral aggregation produced in Yuxi Mountain of Shanxi, about 50 kilometers west of Changhua Town, Lin’an City, Zhejiang Province. The stone has diverse colors, of which the most precious is the color of chicken blood. Changhua stones are roughly divided into four categories including Changhua Chicken-Blood Stone, Changhua Caidong Stone, Changhua Tianhuang Stone, and Changhua Qiaoshi Stone, which are further categorized into more than 200 sub-categories. Moreover, new subcategories continue to emerge.

A representative of Changhua stones, Changhua Chicken-Blood Stone gets its name from its bright red color,

while being as translucent as jade, becoming a “national treasure”. According to the General History of Zhejiang compiled during the reign of Qing Emperor Qianlong, “red spots in the stone look like vermillion, while the blue and purple parts appear like hawksbill shell; however, this adorable stone becomes rare recently.” Deng Sanmu, an eminent Chinese calligrapher, once said that the quality of chicken-blood stones depends on its blood and ground colors. They are the two key factors to assess quality. The brightness and content of the blood color, as well as its shape, distribution and concentration, are important criteria to judge the stone’s quality. For instance, the bright red, red, reddish and blood color can be distributed like slices, belts and spots in the stone. Usually, those containing less than 10 percent of blood color are considered of poor quality, those with less than 30 percent of blood color are normal, those with more than 50 percent are precious, and those with more than 70 percent are rare, popularly called “Dahongpao” (Red Robe).
