After the mid-Ming Dynasty, seal stones began to come into the sight of scholarly seal cutters, and soon became a kind of dominant seal materials for both ordinary people and scholars. By the reign of Emperor Kangxi of the Qing Dynasty, the Shoushan, Qingtian and Changhua stones had been the three most famous stones used to carve seals. Complementing with each other, they are the most important components of Chinese seal stones. In the Qing Dynasty, academic research and appreciation of seal stones gradually gained popularity, creating a massive number of relevant research monographs, such as Research of Imperial Academy Supervisor Wen Zhengming’s Seals, For Xu Rong, Essay on the Qingtian Stone for Lin Shiyuan, Research of the Qingtian Stone, Lin’s Genealogy, Annals of the Shoushan Stone, Records on Stone Observation, Later Records on Stone Observation, Songs for the Shoushan Stone, Ode to Shoushan Inkstone Screen, Shoushan Stone, and General
