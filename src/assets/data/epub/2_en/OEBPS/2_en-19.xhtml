Republic of China period, the seal knob art represented by Fuzhou’s Dongmen and Ximen genres was actually an art formed due to pressure from adverse circumstances. Craftsmen in the Kangxi, Yongzheng and Qianlong reigns were like running on the boundless plain and gave full play to their talent and inspiration with confidence, while those in the late Qing and early Republic of China period overcame plentiful difficulties like crossing ragged mountains with their unprecedented sense of balance and skillful flexibility. The former appeared magnificent and fluent, with a touch of masculinity, just like the painting of the Song Dynasty; and the latter was lovely and graceful, with an ambience of elegance, just like scholarly painting of the Yuan and Ming dynasties. The two strongly contrast with each other, both spiritually and aesthetically, thus giving a true picture of the changes in historical environments and cultural ecosystems of the seal knob carving art.
