Artistic Value: Changhua stone is of diverse cultural and economic value in terms of seal materials, inscriptions, and decorative knobs, evidenced in seal monographs and artworks by famous seal cutters over the ages. Such seal-cutting masters and celebrated figures as Deng Shiru, Zhao Zhiqian, Wu Changshuo, Ke Zhai, Qi Baishi, Guo Moruo, and Sha Menghai all made remarkable achievements for the development of Changhua Chicken-Blood Stone culture. In addition to seals, the stone is also used to carve artworks and ornaments. In modern times, chicken-blood stone carvings have won prizes at many domestic and foreign competitions and played an important role in preserving traditional Chinese art and expanding carving techniques. Changhua stone carvings are of high natural, artistic and cultural beauty–not only tangible artworks, but also demonstrating richness and

inspiring philosophical connotations. Just like beautiful paintings and poems, they showcase the integration of artistic form and spirit.

The Changhua stone integrates natural and artificial beauty, demonstrating a diverse charm for its varied colors, shapes, and textures. Due to its enthralling colors, high quality, beautiful patterns, translucent luster, and cultural profundity, the stone has won favor among countless stone lovers and appreciators. The red color of the Changhua stone can create an immense power of encouragement and inspiration. As a mysterious symbol of authority, the unique vermilion color is believed to have the effect to pray for blessings and dispel the evil.

In a word, the Changhua stone is of great historical, cultural and artistic value and has played a singular role on historical stage over millennia. It has not only facilitated the inheritance of Chinese civilization, but also left the footprints of Chinese culture.
