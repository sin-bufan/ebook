The famous Shoushan stone was already spoken highly by scholars as early as the late Ming and early Qing dynasties. Ruo Gaozhao and Mao Qiling wrote prose and poems in its praise. Moreover, famous sculptors such as Pan Zihe, Yang Yuxuan and Zhao Shangyun left behind many masterpieces made of it. Their skills were passed down through the stone to showcase its unparalleled artistic and cultural connotations. For this reason, the stone entered the mansions of aristocrats and wealthy people, becoming collectables as valuable as antiques, calligraphy and paintings. However, this also resulted in ordinary seal cutters losing their access to the stone. They had to choose rough stones from Laoling, Emei and Dashan in Shoushan or remains from famous stone mines. With these materials, they could not give full play to their carving skills. Consequently, some erroneously assumed

that Shoushan stone was less suitable for seal cutting than Qingtian stone.

Shoushan stone has diverse sub-categories and textures. In terms of minerals, they include dickite, kaolinite, pyrophyllite, gyulekhite, etc. They are either as translucent as jade or as rough as ordinary rocks. Different sub-categories vary in texture and nature, some being tender, some crisp and others soft. Therefore, a seal cutter must be familiar with a stone’s nature and adopt different carving skills based upon their different textures, just like calligraphers and painters carefully choose their ink and paper. A perfect seal is the result of compatibility between stone and carving skills.

The Changhua stone comes from Changhua County, Lin’an City, in Zhejiang Province, and the minerals are mainly dickite and kaolinite.
