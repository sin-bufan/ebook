In regard to seal stone culture, its meaning, I believe, must be found through examining the long history of cultural tradition and the aesthetic activities of Chinese scholars. No matter how beautiful it is, a stone [in its natural state] is just a piece of stone if it has nothing to do with human activities. A seal stone gains cultural connotations only when it is carved, polished, ornamented and appreciated by humankind. In regard to seal stone culture, we are discussing cultural activities, art creations and the aesthetic accomplishments of humankind. The latter plays the decisive role in turning natural gemstones into cultural legacies.
