Postscript

China boasts a time-honored, profound seal stone culture. Since its establishment, the Gems & Jewelry Trade Association of China (GJTAC) has been dedicated to spreading and innovating seal stone culture. A decade ago, Shoushan, Qingtian, Changhua and Balin stones stood out among other seal stones in a competition organized by GJTAC. Through a series of promotion campaigns, GJTAC has accelerated the development of local seal stone culture, considerably enhancing the influence and reputation of the four famous seal stones.

In 2011, GJTAC joined hands with the People’s Government of Jin’an District of Fuzhou City, Fujian Province, of Qingtian County, Zhejiang Province, Lin’an City, Zhejiang Province and Bairin Right Banner of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, to hold the Chinese Seal Cutting Jinshi Awards. Taking advantage of the platform of China International Jewelry Fair that attracted numerous visitors due to its widespread fame, the event showcased seal-cutting masterpieces made of the

four famous stones and promoted China’s traditional seal stone culture. Media organizations including CCTV and Beijing TV Station, as well as many online and print media outlets, covered the event, further enhancing its fame.

Meanwhile, the GJTAC Secretariat has devoted much manpower and energy to soliciting insightful essays to assess prizewinning works at the event, and compiled the book. Featuring informative texts, impressive photos and novel design, this book aims to combine the traditional seal stone culture with modern social thoughts. Thus, it is imbued with both a poetic atmosphere and a strong flavor of everyday life.

We hereby would like to express our gratitude to local governments, associations and enterprises, as well as collectors, carvers and seal cutters, for the support they have provided. It is their kind help that gives us the confidence to continue holding the Chinese Seal Cutting Jinshi Awards. We hope that the event will continue to gather more people in order to become a famous brand in the seal stone industry and make greater contributions to the development of the seal stone culture.
