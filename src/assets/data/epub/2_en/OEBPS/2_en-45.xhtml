The first category is the chicken-blood stone. Mercury sulfide penetrated into the lava of the Balin stone, giving it its distinctive name. Balin Chicken-Blood Stone can be further divided into 24 sub-categories according to the content, degree, texture and patterns of redness, including Xiyanghong, Feicuihong, Caixiahong, Mudanhong, and Furonghong. The chicken-blood stone is so rare that only one piece can be found in 10,000 tons of rough stones; meanwhile, it symbolizes good luck because “chicken” sounds like “auspiciousness” in Chinese and red represents good fortune in Chinese folklore. Therefore, it is widely believed that wearing or collecting chicken-blood stones can bring good fortune and wealth. Its great scarcity determines its high value. A saying prevalent among stone dealers and collectors goes that “each inch of chicken-blood stone is worth three inches of gold.” As a symbol of good fortune, the stone is particularly popular among Chinese. Stone collectors claim that stones inject elegance and dignity into a home, especially the elegant,

rare chicken-blood stones. Dealers assert that a piece of chicken-blood stone is better than thousands of other precious stones, and chicken-blood stone business is the most profitable. In a word, the auspicious, dignified and elegant chicken-blood stone is a treasure admired and desired by many people.

The second category is Fuhuang stone. With boehmite as the main ingredient, it also contains pholerite and a little ferrous element. The stone was first discovered by Liu Fu, head of a mining team. It looks similar to the Tianhuang stone. Balin Fuhuang Stone is translucent and soft. Top-quality Fuhuang stone is pure and almost transparent and as valuable as the high-end chicken-blood stone. As its name suggests, the stone is yellow. Fu means happiness, and Huang pronounces like “emperor” in Chinese. For this reason, the stone symbolizes bliss and dignity and thus has won wide favor among collectors. Balin Fuhuang Stone can be divided into 23 sub-categories including Milahuang and Jiyouhuang.
