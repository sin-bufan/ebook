Collectors actually have magic bond with the stones they collected, which is the so-called “psychic connection.” Such tacit connection is attributed between the affection between collectors and stones. It is stemmed from material things, but finally settles in the faith of God. Gu Huimin once wrote a poem titled Ode to Shoushan Stone: “The king of seal stones in my blur memory are various in appearance; Their unique textures come from erosion of streams, soil and water, but different mine pits have nothing in common. One can know the stone in central Fujian well only when understanding the spirit it symbolizes. Once I thought I owned what I saw, and explained that carving an ox is just like carving an insect.” The idea of “owning what I saw” requires the desire for knowledge and the spirit of open-mindedness, so that one can understand the beauty of the Shoushan stone purely from the aesthetical perspective. Keeping this in mind, collectors of Shoushan stones can get more than just wealth.
