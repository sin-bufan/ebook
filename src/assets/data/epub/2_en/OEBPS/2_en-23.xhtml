There are more than 150 sub-categories of Shoushan stone, of which many kinds of Dongshi stone feature a semi-transparent texture and diverse colors. Some liken Qingtian stone to an elegant maiden who applies light makeup and washes clothes by the river, and Shoushan stone to a princess dignified and graceful. Of all the sub-categories of Shoushan stone, Tianhuang is the most famous. Found in croplands and the gravel bed of the Tianhuang Stream in Shoushan of Fujian Province, it features a smooth, tender texture and gorgeous colors due to long-term water erosion. As soon as it was discovered, it has been considered a kind of stone with royal dignity, favored by emperors and aristocrats in past dynasties. A prevalent saying is that Tianhuang stone is even rarer than gold, hence its nickname “King of Stones”. Stone mines in Shoushan are categorized into field, water and

mountainous pits. The main tone is yellow, ranging from golden and orange to the colors of ripe loquat, laurel flowers and chicken grease. There are also some species of Tianhuang stone in red, white, black, silver with a golden wrapping, and the color of crow’s feathers. All kinds of the Tianhuang stone are precious, with ordinary species priced at more than 1,000 yuan per gram; High-quality Tianhuang stones, which can weigh over 50 grams, can sell for 100,000 yuan per gram. Stones produced in water pits usually glitter with an enthralling luster, and such sub-categories as Yunaodong, Huandong, Niujiaodong, Shuijingdong, and Tianlandong have been popular among collectors since ancient times. It is pity that water pits no longer produce stones today. Mountainous pits produce a variety of stones, however. The Baifurong (“White Lotus”) stone from the General Cave has the color of lotus root, ivory or mutton fat and appears as translucent and smooth as Hotan jade, but is more valuable.
