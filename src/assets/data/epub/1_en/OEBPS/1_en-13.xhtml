Sinosauropteryx

This was the first feathered dinosaur ever discovered. Though it had a coat of feathers alternating with tawny and orange bands covering its body, it was unable to fly. The main function of the feathers was to protect its skin and maintain body temperature.

The dinosaur was a cold-blooded animal while a bird is homothermal (constant, high body temperature). A feathered mini-dinosaur like Sinosauropteryx should be an intermediate species in the evolution from dinosaur to bird.

Living period: Early Cretaceous Period, about 125 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Carnivorous and bipedal locomotion

Classification-Order: Saurischia; Sub-order: Theropoda; Family: Compsognathus
