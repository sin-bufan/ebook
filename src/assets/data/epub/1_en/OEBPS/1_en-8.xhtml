Epidexipteryx

This was one of the tiniest dinosaurs in the world, with a body only 25 centimeters in length,or 45 centimeters if one includes its rectrix (tail feathers).

Epidexipteryx was akin to birds. However, its feathers were only ornamental and could not help it fly. According to scientists, Epidexipteryx would show off its gorgeous feathers in courtship display. Doesn’t it remind you of a charming peacock, often called the “king of birds”?

Living period: Mid-to-late Jurassic Period,about 160 million years ago

Habitat: East of Inner Mongolia; Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Carnivorous, arboreal, and bipedal locomotion

Classification-Order: Saurischia; Sub-order: Theropoda; Family: Scansoriopteryx
