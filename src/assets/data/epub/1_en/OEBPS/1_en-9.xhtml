Archaeopteryx

This gets star treatment among paleontologists. As early as 1860, Archaeopteryx fossils were discovered in Solnhofen, Germany, as well as a fancy feather that has been preserved. The find of Archaeopteryx helped support the evolutionist ideas presented by Charles Darwin in the newly-published On the Origin of Species.

Archaeopteryx was believed to have been an ancestor of birds, but now more and more scientists hold the view that it was not a bird, but a dinosaur.

Living period: Late Jurassic Period, about 145 million years ago

Habitat: Europe

Living habits: Carnivorous; arboreal or epigeal; flying ability

Classification-Order: Aves; Family: Archaeopteryx
