Tianyulong

This was a mini-dinosaur with a one-meter body. It had a coat of fur, with tubular and unforked spiny hairs on its back and tail. Though a magnificent sight, they were not one of the ancestors of birds.

Tianyulong fossil specimens can be found in Shandong Tianyu Museum of Natural History in Linyi City, Shandong Province. That’s how it got its name. It is officially called Confucius-Tianyulong as Linyi is close to Qufu, birthplace of Confucius (551BC–479 BC).

Living period: Mid-to-late Jurassic Period, about 160 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Herbivorous or omnivorous; bipedal locomotion

Classification-Order: Ornithischia; Family: Heterodontosauridae
