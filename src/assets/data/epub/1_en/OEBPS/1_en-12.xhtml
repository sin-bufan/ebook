Yanornis

About the size of a big white pigeon, it was typical of the oldest modern birds. It was able to run fast and was also a good flyer. Yanornis evolved into a modern bird with no striking differences between them. Like seagulls and ospreys, Yanornis fed on fish.

It had a relatively long beak and its remaining teeth were also found in its fossil. In its stomach, scientists found the remains of fish and the gastrolith (stomach stones) rocks for grinding tough food.

Living period: Early Cretaceous Period, about 125 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Carnivorous; able to fly

Classification-Order: Aves; Family: Songlingornis
