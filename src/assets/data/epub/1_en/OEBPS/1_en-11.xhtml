Confuciusornis

This was about the size of a pigeon. It is the oldest known bird with a toothless horny beak. Its coccyx was similar to that of a modern bird and it was more adept at flying.

Morphologically, Confuciousornis had clear sexually distinctions. The male had a pair of long streamer-like tail feathers to attract the attention of the female during courtship display. Biologists call the phenomenon of sexual variation as “sexual dimorphism”. Confuciousornis is the oldest known bird of that kind.

Living period: Early Cretaceous Period, about 125 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Herbivorous and arboreal, able to fly

Classification-Order: Aves; Family: Confuciousornis
