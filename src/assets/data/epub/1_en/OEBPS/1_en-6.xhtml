Wukongopterus

Some might say the “dinosaur” that we are most familiar with would be the pterosaur. However, pterosaurs were not dinosaurs at all, but, rather, reptiles. They were able to ascend skyward 70 million years earlier than birds. When dinosaurs dominated the land, pterosaurs were masters of the sky. Alas, towards the end of the Cretaceous Period, they became extinct along with nearly all other creatures on Earth after a collision with an asteroid caused huge dust clouds that blanked out the sun.

Wukongopterus was a basic type of pterosauria. Isn’t it interesting that it was named by our Chinese paleontologists after the hero Sun Wukong from the novel Journey to the West, one of the four most-read classics in China?

Living period: Mid-to-late Jurassic Period, about 160 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Carnivorous and arboreal

Classification-Order: Pterosauria
