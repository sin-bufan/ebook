Anchiornis

Anchiorn was a small creature, only about 30 centimeters in length, so we call it a mini-dinosaur. It resembled a bird in that it had remex feathers, but it could only manage limited flight. It was the earliest dinosaur that was able to “fly” up into the azure sky. The Anchiorn had feathers of gorgeous hues so as to attract a potential mate. It also used a loud display to be easily identified and able to terrorize rivals in vying for habitat and food. A careful study of it can help you find these traits in the birds of today.

Living period: Mid-to-late Jurassic Period, about 160 million years ago

Habitat: Liaoning Province, China

Living habits: Carnivorous and arboreal

Classification-Order: Maniraptora; Family: Troodontidae
