Dalai Nur

The Dalai Nur Volcanic Group is one of the nine biggest volcanic groups in northeastern China formed between 100,000 and 10,000 years ago and containing over 120 typical craters. As the second largest inland stretch of water in Inner Mongolia, Dalai Nur is hailed as the “Swan Lake of China”. The Haolai, to the south of the lake, is considered the world’s narrowest river.
