Tucked away in the northwestern suburbs of Chifeng City, located on the eastern Inner Mongolia Plateau, is a pure and mysterious holy garden, an art masterpiece created by Nature over millions of years. This is China’s Hexigten Global Geopark dubbed as the “Golden Triangle North of the Great Wall”.

Hexigten Global Geopark features rich geological landscapes and a diversified ecological system. It contains the largest, best developed and preserved Quaternary glacial landforms in eastern China, including fascinating and magnificent granite potholes, the unique “Arshihaty Hoodoos”, the meandering Xar Moron River stretching for hundreds of miles, the vast Dalai Nur and the boundless

Gongger and Ulan Butong grassland soffering much picturesque scenery. On February 11, 2005, this natural “Earth Science Museum” became a member of the Global Geopark Network after UNESCO ratification.

Apart from its spectacular natural scenery, Hexigten Global Geopark also boasts examples of profound ethnic culture and time-honored folk customs. Here, visitors can enjoy fragrant local liquors and gain understanding of life in a Mongolian yurt, colorful ethnic costumes, traditional Mongolian religious culture through the holy Oboo Ceremony, and enthralling song and dance performances.
