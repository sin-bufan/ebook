Otingdag

As one of the four major sandy lands in China, the origins of the Otingdag Sandy Land can be traced back to some point in the Pliocene Epoch between 1.8-5.3 million years ago. There are numerous dunes and colorful shrubs, giving it a reputation as a “Garden in the Desert”. The Bayin Oboo National Nature Reserve in the north of the region is noted for a primitive Picea mongolica forest covering an area of 25.33 square kilometers. This endangered species, which can adapt well to arid soil, has never been found anywhere else in the world. It has become known as a “Living Fossil” and “Biological Gene Bank”.
